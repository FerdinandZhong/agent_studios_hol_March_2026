# Prohibited and Restricted Goods — UK Import Reference

**Source:** HM Revenue & Customs / Border Force — Goods Restriction Reference  
**Applies to:** Goods imported into Great Britain  
**Last reviewed:** January 2024

> **Important:** This document provides an overview for call centre guidance purposes. It is not a comprehensive legal reference. Importers should always consult the relevant government department for definitive guidance.

---

## Prohibited Goods (Absolute Ban)

Prohibited goods may **never** be imported into the UK under any circumstances. Attempting to import them is a criminal offence.

| Category | Examples | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Class A controlled drugs | Heroin, cocaine, MDMA, LSD, methamphetamine | Seizure + criminal prosecution (up to 14 years imprisonment) |
| Offensive weapons | Flick knives, gravity knives, zombie knives, knuckle dusters, certain martial arts weapons | Seizure + prosecution |
| Indecent images of children | Any media containing such material | Seizure + prosecution |
| Counterfeit currency | Fake banknotes or coins of any currency | Seizure + prosecution |
| Rabies-susceptible pets (without compliance) | Dogs, cats, ferrets from non-listed countries without rabies vaccination + microchip | Seizure + quarantine |
| Endangered species without CITES | Live specimens, skins, ivory, body parts of species listed under CITES Appendix I | Seizure + prosecution |

## Restricted Goods (Require Licence or Permit)

Restricted goods **can** be imported but require a licence, permit, or certificate before or at the time of import. Goods imported without the required authorisation will be seized.

---

### Category 1: Controlled Drugs (Schedule 2 & 3)

Pharmaceuticals and research chemicals that are controlled under the Misuse of Drugs Act can be imported under a **Home Office Import Licence**.

**Examples:** Morphine, codeine, some benzodiazepines, amphetamine-based medications.

**How to apply:** Home Office — Drugs Licensing and Compliance Unit  
**Lead time:** 10–15 working days

---

### Category 2: Firearms and Weapons

All firearms, component parts, and ammunition require a **Firearms Import Licence** from HMRC.

Antique firearms (manufactured before 1899) are generally exempt, but must be assessed by the Force Firearms Licensing team.

**Deactivated firearms:** Must comply with UK deactivation standards and carry an EU/UK proof house deactivation certificate. Post-Brexit standards require re-deactivation if the original work was done to EU standards.

---

### Category 3: Dual-Use Goods

**Dual-use goods** are items designed for civilian use but with potential military or WMD applications. They are controlled under the Export Control Act 2002 (applied in reverse for imports).

**Examples:**
- High-performance computing equipment (certain processor speeds)
- Precision machine tools (above controlled thresholds)
- Encryption software and hardware
- Certain chemicals (precursors to chemical weapons)
- Electronic test equipment used in missile guidance

**Licensing authority:** Export Control Joint Unit (ECJU)  
**Assessment process:** Submit an application describing the goods in technical detail, end-user, and intended use. ECJU may consult the Foreign Office.

---

### Category 4: Food and Agricultural Products (Plant and Animal Health)

Goods of animal or plant origin require sanitary and phytosanitary (SPS) controls. Specific requirements depend on the country of origin and product type.

**For goods of animal origin (meat, fish, dairy, eggs, honey):**
- Must enter through a **Border Control Post (BCP)**
- Require an **Export Health Certificate (EHC)** issued by the exporting country's veterinary authority
- Subject to identity checks, document checks, and physical inspection at BCP

**For live plants and plant products:**
- Must be accompanied by a **Phytosanitary Certificate** from the national plant protection organisation of the exporting country
- High-risk plants (e.g., from certain countries/species) are subject to enhanced checks

**For organic goods:**
- Must carry an **Organic Certificate** from an approved certification body
- The exporter must be approved by the relevant authority in the exporting country

---

### Category 5: Textiles — Origin and Quota Controls

Certain textile imports are subject to safeguard measures, quota limits, or country-specific restrictions.

**Anti-dumping duties:**  
Some textile imports from China and other countries attract **anti-dumping duties** in addition to standard customs duty. These are listed in the UK Trade Remedies Authority (TRA) schedule. Check the UK Global Tariff tool for any additional measures on your specific HS code.

**Forced labour provisions:**  
From 2024, goods produced wholly or partly using forced labour may be prohibited from import under the **Import of Goods (Prohibition) Regulations**. This particularly affects cotton goods from specific regions. Importers must be prepared to demonstrate supply chain due diligence.

**Labelling requirements:**  
Textile goods sold in Great Britain must comply with the **Textile Products (Labelling and Fibre Composition) Regulations 2012**. Labels must state the fibre composition and, for care instructions, follow EN ISO 3758. Non-compliant labelling can prevent market release.

---

### Category 6: Electronics and Technology

**UKCA Marking:**  
From 1 January 2023, most regulated electronic and electrical products placed on the Great Britain market must carry the **UKCA (UK Conformity Assessed)** marking. This replaces the former CE marking for GB use. Products still carrying only CE marking require a UK approved body Declaration of Conformity.

**Regulated product categories requiring UKCA:**
- Electrical equipment (low voltage)
- Radio equipment (radios, WiFi devices, Bluetooth)
- Toys
- Personal protective equipment
- Pressure vessels

**Smartphones and laptops:** Generally exempt from import duty (HS 8471, 8517 — 0% MFN rate), but must comply with UKCA or CE marking rules for sale.

**Radio Frequency Spectrum:**  
Radio transmitting equipment must be type-approved for UK frequencies. Non-compliant transmitters (designed for US or Asian frequency bands) cannot be legally used in the UK even if they clear customs.

---

### Category 7: Chemicals (REACH)

Chemicals imported into the UK for commercial use are subject to **UK REACH (Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals)** regulations. Chemical substances that are new to the UK market, or for which no registration exists, require pre-registration and registration with the Health and Safety Executive (HSE).

**Substances of Very High Concern (SVHCs):** Subject to authorisation requirements. Check the SVHC candidate list on the HSE website.

---

### Category 8: Endangered Species (CITES)

Import of species listed under the **Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (CITES)** requires CITES import and export permits.

**Appendix I species** (most endangered): Commercial import is generally prohibited. Permits may be issued for non-commercial purposes (scientific research, personal pets pre-owned).

**Appendix II species:** Commercial import is permitted with valid CITES import and export permits.

**Common mistakes:**
- Coral, shells, and seahorses are CITES-listed and commonly imported without permits
- Certain exotic timbers (rosewood, ebony) are CITES-controlled — furniture importers must verify
- Musical instruments made from protected woods (e.g., Brazilian rosewood guitar fretboards) require CITES documentation

**UK CITES authority:** Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA)

## Sanctions and Embargoes

Goods cannot be imported from countries or entities subject to UK financial sanctions or arms embargoes. Current UK sanctions regimes include:

| Country / Regime | Relevant restrictions |
|---|---|
| Russia | Comprehensive goods, technology, and luxury goods embargo (post-Feb 2022) |
| Belarus | Import ban on various goods including textiles, cement, iron and steel |
| Iran | Arms embargo; controls on nuclear-related goods |
| North Korea | Comprehensive embargo — virtually all goods prohibited |
| Myanmar | Arms embargo; luxury goods restrictions |
| Syria | Wide-ranging goods and technology restrictions |

**Sanctions screening:** Importers must screen their suppliers, shippers, and vessels against the UK sanctions list (OFSI consolidated list) before importing. Sanctions violations are strict liability — OFSI does not need to prove intent.

**Vessel screening:** Check whether the vessel carrying your goods appears on OFSI or UN vessel watchlists, particularly for Russia/Belarus-origin goods where indirect routing is common.

## Quick Reference: Should I Be Worried?

| If your goods are... | Check... |
|---|---|
| Textiles from Bangladesh, Cambodia, Myanmar | REX statement or Form A — to claim 0% duty |
| Electronics (laptops, phones, tablets) | UKCA/CE marking compliance |
| Chemicals (any commercial volume) | UK REACH registration |
| Food or agricultural products | Border Control Post, Export Health Certificate |
| Any goods from Russia, Belarus, Iran, North Korea | Sanctions / embargo status |
| Furniture or instruments from tropical hardwoods | CITES listing of wood species |
| Protective workwear or PPE | UKCA marking, EN standard certifications |
| Exotic animals, skins, shells, coral | CITES permits (even as decorative items) |
