# HS Chapter 61 — Textile Classification Rules and Duty Rates

**Source:** World Customs Organization (WCO) Harmonized System 2022 Edition — Chapter 61 Notes  
**Applicability:** UK Global Tariff (post-Brexit); EU Combined Nomenclature; ASEAN AHTN  
**Document Type:** Trade Compliance Knowledge Base — Classification Reference  

---

## Chapter 61 Overview

Chapter 61 covers knitted or crocheted clothing articles and clothing accessories. It is one of the highest-risk HS chapters for misclassification fraud because:

- Duty rates vary significantly between headings (e.g., 12% for T-shirts vs. 6.5% for industrial clothing)
- The fibre composition (cotton vs. man-made fibre vs. blends) determines the subheading and duty rate
- Gender attribution (men's/boys' vs. women's/girls') can affect classification in some regimes
- Fraudsters frequently declare higher-duty goods under lower-duty headings or as industrial/protective clothing

## HS 6109 — T-Shirts, Singlets and Other Vests (Detail)

### Classification Criteria

Goods classified under **6109** must be:
1. **Knitted or crocheted** (not woven — woven T-shirts fall under Chapter 62)
2. **T-shirts, singlets, or vests** — garments with or without sleeves, fitting close to the body at the torso, not extending below the hip
3. Distinguished from **shirts (6105/6106)** by absence of a collar or button placket

### Subheading Structure

| Subheading | Description |
|---|---|
| **6109.10** | Of cotton |
| **6109.20** | Of man-made fibres |
| **6109.90** | Of other textile materials |

**Fibre determination:** The composition is determined by the chief weight rule. A garment with 60% cotton and 40% polyester is classified under **6109.10** (cotton).

### UK Duty Rate for 6109.10
- **Standard (MFN) rate:** 12.0% CIF
- **UK Generalised System of Preferences (UK-GSP) — Enhanced Framework (Bangladesh):** 0% (with valid Form A / REX declaration)
- **Preferential duty saving per shipment (10,000 pcs at £3.20 avg price):** approximately £3,840 in avoided duty — significant incentive for fraud

## Known Misclassification Patterns for Chapter 61

### Pattern 1: Upward Misclassification (Lower Duty Heading)
Goods that are clearly T-shirts (6109) are declared as:
- **6113** (coated fabric garments — 6.5% duty) — claimed to have rubberised or waterproof coating
- **6217** (other clothing accessories) — claimed to be "promotional items" or "accessories"
- **Chapter 63** (other made-up textile articles) — declared as rags or cleaning cloths

**Detection signal:** Product description uses vague language ("textile articles", "fabric goods") instead of naming the garment type.

### Pattern 2: Fibre Content Misrepresentation
Man-made fibre garments (6109.20, 12.0%) declared as cotton (6109.10, 12.0%) — same rate under UK tariff but different under EU tariff (EU applies specific duty per piece for 6109.20 in some GSP frameworks).

### Pattern 3: Chapter 62 vs Chapter 61 Switching
Woven cotton T-shirts (Chapter 62, heading 6205) correctly attract 12% duty. Fraudsters may declare under 6109 to falsely claim GSP preference for Bangladesh, where 6109 is covered but the specific woven item may fail Rules of Origin tests.

## Verification Checklist for Customs Officers

When examining a shipment declared under **6109.10** from Bangladesh:

- [ ] Confirm garment is knitted/crocheted (not woven) — check fabric structure
- [ ] Verify fibre content label matches declaration (≥ 50% cotton for 6109.10)
- [ ] Confirm garment style matches T-shirt/vest description (no collar, no full-length button placket)
- [ ] Check declared unit price against HMRC benchmark (2024 benchmark: USD 3.20/pc ± USD 0.65)
- [ ] Verify Form A or REX statement is attached if GSP rate is claimed
- [ ] Cross-check origin declaration — Bangladesh must meet Rules of Origin (double transformation or 50% local value added)
- [ ] Inspect quantity — count carton marks against packing list

## Common Fraud Indicators for HS 6109.10

| Indicator | Threshold | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Declared unit price < USD 1.80 | Below minimum observed price | Mandatory valuation query |
| Declared unit price < USD 1.60 | 50% below benchmark mean | Refer to fraud unit |
| Price deviation > 2 standard deviations below mean | > 75% below benchmark | Under-invoicing flag — hold for valuation |
| No Form A / REX statement with GSP rate claimed | Any occurrence | Reject GSP preference — apply MFN rate |
| Vague product description (no fibre content, no style) | Any occurrence | Request detailed sample or physical inspection |
| Shipping route via Port Klang, Colombo, or Dubai | Any occurrence | Origin-washing risk — request proof of origin |
