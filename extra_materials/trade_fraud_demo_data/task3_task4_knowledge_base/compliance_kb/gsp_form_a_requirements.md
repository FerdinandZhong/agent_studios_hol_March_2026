# GSP Certificate of Origin (Form A) — Requirements for Bangladesh Exports to the UK

**Source:** UK Department for International Trade — UK Generalised Scheme of Preferences (UK-GSP), January 2022  
**Applicability:** Bangladesh exports to the United Kingdom under the Enhanced Preferences Framework  
**Document Type:** Trade Compliance Knowledge Base — Certificate Requirements  

---

## Overview

The **UK Generalised System of Preferences (UK-GSP)** grants reduced or zero import duty rates on goods imported from developing countries. Bangladesh is classified under the **Enhanced Preferences Framework (EPF)**, the highest tier of the UK-GSP, which provides:

- **Zero-rate duty (0%)** on most manufactured goods including textiles and clothing (HS Chapters 50–63)
- Requires proof that goods originate in Bangladesh per the applicable Rules of Origin

Without a valid GSP proof of origin, goods are assessed at the **Most Favoured Nation (MFN) standard rate** (12% for HS 6109.10), resulting in significantly higher duty liability.

---

## Proof of Origin Documents

### Option A: Form A (GSP Certificate of Origin)

The **Form A** is the traditional paper-based certificate of origin for GSP purposes. It must be:

1. **Issued by an authorised issuing body** in Bangladesh:
   - Export Promotion Bureau (EPB) of Bangladesh
   - Bangladesh Garment Manufacturers and Exporters Association (BGMEA) — for garments only
   - Bangladesh Knitwear Manufacturers and Exporters Association (BKMEA) — for knitwear

2. **Completed in full** — all mandatory boxes must be filled:
   - Box 1: Goods consigned from (Exporter's business name, address, country)
   - Box 2: Goods consigned to (Consignee's name, country, reference)
   - Box 3: Means of transport and route (vessel name, bill of lading number)
   - Box 4: For official use (customs stamp)
   - Box 5: Item number
   - Box 6: Marks and numbers of packages
   - Box 7: Number and kind of packages; description of goods (must match invoice)
   - Box 8: Origin criterion (P = wholly produced; W = sufficient working/processing)
   - Box 9: Gross weight or other quantity
   - Box 10: Invoice number and date
   - Box 11: Certification (issuing authority stamp + signature)
   - Box 12: Declaration by the exporter

3. **Submitted to UK customs** at time of importation (or within 2 years if retrospective claim)

4. **Cover the specific shipment** — the invoice number on the Form A must match the commercial invoice

### Option B: Registered Exporter (REX) System Statement

Since 2019, UK-GSP eligible exporters may use the **REX system** instead of Form A. A registered exporter makes a **statement on origin** directly on the commercial invoice or any commercial document:

```
"The exporter of the products covered by this document (UK REX No. [XXXXXXXXXX]) declares 
that, except where otherwise clearly indicated, these products are of [BANGLADESH] preferential 
origin."
```

- REX registration is free and valid indefinitely unless revoked
- The exporter must be registered with the Bangladesh customs authorities
- The statement must appear on the invoice or a separate declaration accompanying the shipment

## Rules of Origin for UK-GSP Enhanced Preferences (Bangladesh Textiles)

For **HS Chapter 61** (knitted clothing), Bangladesh must satisfy the **double transformation rule** OR the **alternative value-added threshold**:

### Rule 1: Double Transformation (Primary Rule)
Goods classified under Chapter 61 must be produced from:
- **Yarn** (not fabric) — the manufacturing must involve at least two processing stages:
  1. Spinning (yarn from fibre)
  2. Knitting + garment making (fabric from yarn → garment)

Bangladesh satisfies double transformation if knitting and garment manufacturing both occur in Bangladesh, even if the raw cotton is imported.

### Rule 2: Value-Added Threshold (Alternative Rule)
Where double transformation cannot be demonstrated, the ex-works (EXW) price of the final product must consist of **at least 50% locally added value**:

```
Local Value Added % = (EXW Price − Value of Non-Originating Materials) / EXW Price × 100
```

If the declared unit price of USD 0.80 is used as the EXW basis and raw materials (imported cotton yarn) cost ~USD 0.65 per unit, the local value added = (0.80 − 0.65) / 0.80 = 18.75% — **far below the 50% threshold**. This is a strong indicator that either:
- The price is under-declared, or
- The goods do not genuinely originate in Bangladesh under this rule

**Either condition disqualifies the GSP claim.**

## Common Form A Fraud and Errors

| Fraud Type | Description | Detection Method |
|---|---|---|
| **False Form A** | Counterfeit certificate not issued by EPB/BGMEA/BKMEA | Verify serial number with issuing body online portal |
| **Country-of-origin washing** | Goods manufactured in China or Vietnam transhipped through Bangladesh with false Form A | Check shipping route; verify factory registration with BGMEA/BKMEA |
| **Mismatch between Form A and invoice** | Invoice number, quantity, or value on Form A differs from commercial invoice | Compare Box 10 (Form A) against invoice |
| **Insufficient transformation** | Chinese-made fabric cut and sewn in Bangladesh — only one transformation stage | Request production audit; check yarn purchase records |
| **Backdated certificate** | Form A dated before goods were manufactured | Cross-reference with production records and ship date |
| **Altered certificate** | Original Form A fields over-typed or corrected without issuing body counter-signature | Physical inspection of original document |

## Key Risk Signals in the Demo Shipment (INV-2024-00123)

The shipment **INV-2024-00123** (Textile House Ltd, Bangladesh → ABC Trading UK, UK) exhibits the following Form A issues:

1. **Form A is NOT submitted** — the certificate is listed as "NOT SUBMITTED" in the accompanying document checklist. The importer is implicitly claiming the standard MFN rate (duty payable) or is relying on an unverified claim.

2. **No REX statement** on the commercial invoice — no REX registration number appears in the invoice text.

3. **Value-added test fails** — at the declared price of USD 0.80/pc, the goods cannot meet the 50% local value-added threshold required as an alternative rule. The declared value is 75% below benchmark, meaning the FOB cost structure cannot support the 50% threshold.

4. **Missing Rules of Origin declaration** — Box 12 of Form A (exporter's declaration that goods meet RoO) has not been completed because no Form A has been submitted.

**Compliance determination:** GSP preference **cannot be granted**. MFN duty rate of **12% CIF** applies. On a CIF value of USD 8,502.00, the duty liability is **USD 1,020.24** rather than the USD 0 that would apply under GSP. Duty under-payment risk: **USD 1,020.24** per shipment.

At the frequency of shipments observed (47 shipments in the last 2 years), cumulative duty under-payment risk: **~USD 47,951**.
