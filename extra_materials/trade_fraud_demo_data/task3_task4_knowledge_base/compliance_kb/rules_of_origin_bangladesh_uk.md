# Rules of Origin — Bangladesh to United Kingdom (UK-GSP Enhanced Preferences)

**Source:** UK Developing Countries Trading Scheme (DCTS) — Enhanced Preferences Framework  
**Effective Date:** 19 June 2023 (replacing the transitional DCTS arrangements of June 2022)  
**Applicability:** All goods exported from Bangladesh to the United Kingdom under preferential tariff  
**Document Type:** Trade Compliance Knowledge Base — Rules of Origin Reference  

---

## 1. Framework Overview

Bangladesh qualifies for **Enhanced Preferences** under the UK Developing Countries Trading Scheme (DCTS), the UK's post-Brexit replacement for the EU's GSP. Enhanced Preferences provide:

- Zero rate of import duty on **all** manufactured goods (HS Chapters 25–97) except a small exclusion list
- More flexible Rules of Origin than the standard preferences tier
- Cumulation rights with other DCTS-eligible countries

For textiles and clothing (HS Sections XI and XII, Chapters 50–63), the preferential Rules of Origin under UK-DCTS Enhanced Preferences are materially more generous than those applied under the EU GSP scheme.

---

## 2. Product-Specific Rules for Textiles and Clothing

### General Rule for Chapter 61 (Knitted Clothing)

| Chapter | Product | Rule | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 61 | Knitted or crocheted clothing | **CTH + double transformation** OR **value added ≥ 50% EXW** | Primary: Change of Tariff Heading from yarn (Ch. 54–55) with knitting AND making-up in Bangladesh. Alternative: ≥50% local value added at ex-works price |

### Specific Rules for Key Headings

**HS 6109 (T-shirts, singlets):**
- Primary rule: Manufacture from **yarn** (not fabric). Cotton yarn may be imported; spinning need not occur in Bangladesh provided knitting AND cutting/sewing occur there.
- Alternative: Ex-works price has local value added ≥ 50% of the ex-works price of the finished product.

**HS 6203/6204 (Trousers):**
- Same rules as 6109 (CTH from yarn or 50% VAT).

**HS 6110 (Jerseys/pullovers):**
- Same rules as 6109.

## 3. Cumulation Provisions

Under UK-DCTS Enhanced Preferences, Bangladesh benefits from:

### Bilateral Cumulation
Materials originating in the UK may be used in production in Bangladesh and treated as originating in Bangladesh for the purpose of Rules of Origin.

### Extended Cumulation (DCTS Regional Cumulation)
Bangladesh may also cumulate with other DCTS-eligible countries in South/Southeast Asia, including:
- India
- Sri Lanka
- Nepal
- Pakistan (where applicable)

This means cotton yarn sourced from India (a DCTS-eligible country) is treated as "originating" for purposes of the double transformation rule when knitting and garment-making occur in Bangladesh.

**Practical impact:** A Bangladesh garment maker importing Indian cotton yarn still qualifies for UK-DCTS preferential origin provided knitting and making-up occur in Bangladesh, even though the yarn itself is not Bangladesh-origin.

---

## 4. Verification of Double Transformation — Evidence Requirements

Customs authorities may request evidence that double transformation was performed in Bangladesh. The following documents serve as evidence:

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Yarn purchase records** | Prove that production started from yarn (not fabric), confirming the double transformation starting point |
| **Knitting machine records** | Show that knitting occurred in Bangladesh (machine ownership, production logs) |
| **Factory floor plan / production records** | Confirm both knitting and garment making processes exist at the Bangladesh site |
| **BGMEA/BKMEA factory registration certificate** | Confirms legitimate registered operation |
| **Third-party factory audit report** | Independent verification of production process |
| **Bill of materials** | Shows quantities and origins of all inputs (yarn, thread, accessories) |

## 5. Value-Added Calculation Method

Where a supplier cannot demonstrate double transformation, the **50% value-added threshold** applies:

```
Origin-conferring Value Added (%) =

  [ EXW price of finished product − CIF value of all non-originating materials ]
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────  × 100
                        EXW price of finished product

Must be ≥ 50% for preferential origin to be granted.
```

### Example Calculation — INV-2024-00123

| Item | Value (USD/piece) |
|---|---|
| Declared EXW price | 0.80 |
| Imported cotton yarn (non-originating if sourced outside DCTS countries) | ~0.42 |
| Other non-originating inputs (buttons, labels, thread) | ~0.06 |
| Total non-originating materials | ~0.48 |
| Local value added | 0.80 − 0.48 = **0.32** |
| Local value added % | 0.32 / 0.80 = **40%** |
| **Origin determination** | **FAILS — below 50% threshold** |

If the **true market price** (USD 3.20/pc) were used as the EXW basis:

| Item | Value (USD/piece) |
|---|---|
| True EXW price | ~2.80 (adjusted for freight/insurance) |
| Imported cotton yarn (DCTS cumulation with India) | ~0.42 |
| Other non-originating inputs | ~0.06 |
| Total non-originating materials | ~0.48 |
| Local value added | 2.80 − 0.48 = **2.32** |
| Local value added % | 2.32 / 2.80 = **82.9%** |
| **Origin determination** | **PASSES** |

**This confirms that the artificially low declared price (USD 0.80) undermines the Rules of Origin calculation — a secondary effect of under-invoicing that is rarely identified by importers.**

## 6. Origin-Washing via Third Countries

### Definition
Origin-washing is the fraudulent practice of routing goods through an intermediate country to falsely attribute a different country of origin. Common in the Bangladesh-UK textile corridor:

**Scheme A — China → Bangladesh labelling:**
Garments manufactured in China are shipped to Bangladesh, relabelled with "Made in Bangladesh" tags, and re-exported. The goods then claim Bangladesh preferential origin (0% duty) instead of the applicable MFN rate for China (12%).

**Scheme B — Bangladesh → Dubai → UK (route laundering):**
Goods are routed via Dubai or another free trade zone where they undergo minimal processing or repackaging. A new certificate of origin is fraudulently obtained in the transit country.

### Detection Signals

| Signal | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Short production lead time vs. declared origin | Chinese-speed turnaround times (10–14 days) are inconsistent with Bangladeshi manufacturing capacity timelines |
| Absence of BGMEA/BKMEA factory registration | Legitimate Bangladesh garment exporters are registered; absence is suspicious |
| Bill of lading shows prior leg (China → Bangladesh → UK) | Multi-leg routing visible in shipping records |
| Port of loading is a free zone (Jebel Ali, Port Klang FTZ) | Transshipment hub known for origin document manipulation |
| Declared unit price inconsistent with Bangladesh labour costs | Price too low even for Bangladesh (< USD 1.60) or too high without premium branding |

---

## 7. Summary: Rules of Origin Compliance Checklist

For a Bangladesh textile shipment claiming UK-DCTS Enhanced Preferences:

- [ ] Double transformation confirmed (knitting + garment making in Bangladesh) **OR** value added ≥ 50%
- [ ] Form A issued by EPB/BGMEA/BKMEA with all boxes completed **OR** REX statement on invoice
- [ ] Invoice number on Form A matches the commercial invoice exactly
- [ ] Declared price consistent with market benchmark (> USD 1.80 minimum for 6109.10)
- [ ] No evidence of transshipment via origin-washing hub
- [ ] Factory is registered with BGMEA (knitwear) or BKMEA (if applicable)
- [ ] Yarn sourcing documented (Bangladesh-origin or DCTS-cumulation-eligible country)
