## Overview

This document compiles seizure and enforcement case summaries relevant to the Bangladesh–UK textile import corridor (primary commodity: HS Chapter 61, knitted garments) over the period January 2022 to February 2024. The records are sourced from Border Force operational reports and HMRC post-clearance audit findings.

Key trend: The Bangladesh–UK corridor accounts for **18% of all textile seizure cases** handled by the National Targeting Centre in this period, second only to the China–UK corridor (41%). The primary fraud type detected is under-invoicing for customs duty evasion, often combined with missing or fraudulent GSP certificates.

### Case SEZ-2023-FEL-1722
**Port:** Felixstowe  
**Date:** 14 June 2023  
**HS Code:** 6110.20  
**Shipper:** Textile House Ltd, Chittagong, Bangladesh  
**Consignee:** ABC Trading UK Ltd  
**Broker:** FastClear Logistics Ltd  
**Declared Value:** USD 4,500 (10,000 pcs × USD 0.45) — declared as "cotton rags/worn clothing" (HS 6310.90, 0% duty)  
**Outcome:** Physical inspection confirmed goods were new cotton jerseys/pullovers (HS 6110.20, 12% duty), not worn clothing. **Deliberate misclassification** confirmed. Seizure of full consignment; goods released after payment of full duty + 50% penalty = **GBP 8,250 total liability**. Case referred to HMRC Fraud Investigation Service.  
**Notes:** This is a confirmed HS code fraud case (reclassification from 6310.90 to 6110.20). The broker (FastClear Logistics) claimed no knowledge of misclassification. Investigation found the Customs Import Declaration had been filed by FastClear using description provided by the importer without verification.

## Pattern Analysis: Split Shipment Scheme (2024 Q1)

Analysis of trade declarations data reveals a **split shipment pattern** for Textile House Ltd → ABC Trading UK during Q1 2024:

| Date | Declaration | Value (USD) | Quantity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 24 Jan 2024 | INV-2024-00074 | 760 | 1,000 pcs | Below £600 duty-free CIF threshold |
| 31 Jan 2024 | INV-2024-00081 | 800 | 1,000 pcs | Below threshold |
| 07 Feb 2024 | INV-2024-00088 | 770 | 1,000 pcs | Below threshold |
| 14 Feb 2024 | INV-2024-00095 | 790 | 1,000 pcs | Below threshold |
| 21 Feb 2024 | INV-2024-00102 | 820 | 1,000 pcs | Below threshold |
| 28 Feb 2024 | INV-2024-00109 | 780 | 1,000 pcs | Below threshold |
| 07 Mar 2024 | INV-2024-00116 | 7,500 | 10,000 pcs | Exceeds threshold — possible scheme adjustment |

**Assessment:** 6 of 7 shipments in the 45-day window are valued below USD 875 (equivalent to approximately £600-700 CIF at then-current exchange rate, below HMRC's de minimis relief threshold for low-value imports). The timing (approximately 7-day intervals) and the consistent below-threshold values are consistent with a deliberate **split shipment strategy** to avoid duty threshold triggers.

The following week's declaration (INV-2024-00116) at USD 7,500 breaks the pattern — possibly because a large standing order required a single consolidated shipment. This shipment was cleared by OFF-0042 without query.

**Total goods value consolidated from 7 shipments:** USD 11,920 declared; estimated true value: ~USD 77,000; estimated duty evasion: **GBP 7,860**.

## Officer-Broker Collusion Investigation Summary

**Case Reference:** HMRC-IC-2024-0117  
**Officers Under Review:** OFF-0042 (James Whitmore), FastClear Logistics Ltd (Director: David Morrison)  
**Opened:** January 2024 (triggered by SEZ-2024-FEL-0041)  

**Key findings to date:**
- Officer OFF-0042 processed **34 clearances** for FastClear Logistics Ltd in a 12-month period, compared to an average of **5.2 clearances** per officer for the same broker
- Processing time for FastClear/Textile House Ltd shipments averaged **1.1 hours** compared to the port average of **3.8 hours** for comparable textile shipments — suggesting minimal scrutiny
- All 34 clearances resulted in "NONE" inspection type — no documentary checks, no physical inspections
- 28 of 34 shipments were flagged as HIGH anomaly by the automated risk system but were manually overridden to clear without action

**Status:** Active investigation; referral to HMRC Fraud Investigation Service (FIS) under consideration; matter also referred to the Independent Office for Police Conduct (analogous process for customs officers).
